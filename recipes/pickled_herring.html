Pickled Herring

Preserving food was essential for surviving the harsh nordic winters. Pickled herring is a classic example of nordic ingenuity in food preservation.

This tangy fish is perfect as a snack or part of a larger meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb fresh herring, cleaned and filleted
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tbsp each of sugar and salt
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced

Steps

  1. In a saucepan, combine vinegar, water, sugar, salt, and peppercorns. Bring to a boil.
  2. Place the herring fillets in a jar and layer with the sliced onion.
  3. Pour the hot vinegar mixture over the herring until fully covered.
  4. Seal the jar and refrigerate for at least 48 hours before eating.
